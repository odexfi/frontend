How ODEX Works

ODEX is a decentralized exchange built on a public blockchain, utilizing smart contracts and powered by the Ethereum virtual machine.

It employs a modern take on the traditional limit order book model, designed for blockchain environments. This enables traders to place orders at specific buy or sell prices for more efficient trading.

The permissionless infrastructure enables users to deploy a new ODEX order book for any asset pair. Each order book has limited slots for bids and asks, with leading bids and asks overwriting less competitive and optimizing towards price and volume.

Progressive Web dApp

ODEX also offers locally hosted multi-wallets that act as separate, isolated accounts for different trading strategies. These virtual wallets offer a seamless, efficient trading experience where the user, and only the user, has full control of their assets.

By using a PWA frontend ODEX can bypass the 30% commission rates on cryptocurrency payments being enforced by mobile app stores. This makes it possible for anyone with an Android or iPhone to trade anytime, anywhere.

Read more: PWA docs

DeFi Limit Order Book

Limit order books offer users maximal control over their trades, allowing them to specify the exact price at which they are willing to buy or sell an asset. This results in more efficient price discovery and prevents many of the issues with frontrunning and MEV bots.

With Ethereum layer 2 rollups, frequent and granular updates of market prices are possible, leading to more efficient trading and a more intuitive user experience.

Limit order books also offer traders more advanced trading options such as the placement of limit orders, market orders, stop-loss and take-profit orders, providing the trader with a more sophisticated, customizable trading strategy and execution.
